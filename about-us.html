William Das
William Das is a junior at Hunter College High School, passionate about applying his skills in computer science and technology to the real-world. He taught himself how to program at the age of 12, mastering web-based programming languages and later dynamic frameworks in Python and data science. Apart from building websites and managing projects for CFI, William has used his skills in computer science to develop advanced machine learning models to analyze complex biomedical data. He worked on a project with his fellow co-founder Shubh Khanna to create a new way to diagnose ADHD by analyzing variation in eye movements — he is currently seeking a patent, as well as medical approval to test his application in local institutions and hospitals.

Jeremy Kim
Jeremy Kim is a senior at Hunter College High School who will be attending Yale University in the fall. He has worked on an array of deep learning projects, many of which he has presented at science competitions, and has done professional work for businesses through internships. He was named a 2020 Regeneron STS Scholar for his work in solving a classic inverse problem through a neural network-based approach, and plans to continue computer science research in college.
